The French Revolution, which began in 1789 and lasted until the late 1790s, was a watershed period in modern European history. Driven by widespread discontent with the absolute monarchy, astronomical national debt, and the rigid social hierarchy of the Three Estates, French citizens rose up to challenge traditional power structures. They demanded liberty, equality, and fraternity. The revolution witnessed key historic events, including the storming of the Bastille, the execution of King Louis XVI and Marie Antoinette, and the infamous Reign of Terror led by Maximilien Robespierre. Ultimately, the revolution dismantled the ancient feudal system and paved the way for the rise of Napoleon Bonaparte, permanently reshaping global political ideologies.
