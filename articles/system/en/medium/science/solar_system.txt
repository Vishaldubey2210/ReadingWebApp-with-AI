Our solar system consists of the Sun and everything that travels around it. This includes eight major planets, many dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. The Sun is a massive star located at the center of the system, and its gravity pulls all the planets to orbit around it. The four planets closest to the Sun are Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars. They are mostly made of rock and metal. The outer four planets are Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune, which are giant spheres made of gas and ice. Earth is the only known planet that supports life, thanks to its water and atmosphere.
